CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST

Tyler, the Creator

The city and the world become a canvas, an open art gallery through Tyler's concept. Art becomes an exploration of self-identity, shaped by locations that represent reinvention, escape, romance, and personal evolution. The pacing of the songs, the energy, and the album's lost-and-found theme echo city rhythms like traffic, transit, and nightlife.

Verano Sin Ti

Bad Bunny

The album bridges urban spaces from Puerto Rico's coastline to cities like Milwaukee and Los Angeles. Across genres—reggaeton, trap Latino, dancehall, indie rock, electro-pop, and perreo club beats—the project treats the city as a soundscape more than a backdrop. Block parties, late-night drives, and crowded streets fuel every track.

Born Sinner

J. Cole

Rooted in his journey from Fayetteville to New York City, the album uses the city as a mirror for ambition, temptation, and redemption. Urban environments push him toward success while forcing him to confront guilt, faith, and humility. The golden crown becomes a halo and a warning all at once.
